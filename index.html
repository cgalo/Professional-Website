More About Me

I'm looking to apply my programming, mathematical, and technical skills as an intern in the field of computer science.

Hola!

In 2015, I started my undergraduate career at North Park University where I was a student-athlete majoring in Physics & Engineering. I was part of the cross country team as well as the track & field team. North Park is where I fell in love with coding and economics and decided to transfer to the University of Toledo to pursue my passion in both, majoring in computer science with a minor in economics. I started at UToledo in the Spring of 2017 and my expected graduation date is December 2020.

Since then, I have been able to be part of different groups that have helped me develop as a person and as a professional. Currently, I'm an active member of the professional business fraternity, Pi Sigma Epsilon. This is where I've had the honor to grow my business skills, create a bigger network of connections, and collaborate on projects to further improvement of our fraternity and its members. Then there is ACM, Association for Computer Machinery, where I've learned many technical skills. Some of these skills include web development, working as a coding team through git and agile.

Presently I'm an intern at a Associated Spring Raymond as an Information Technology Intern, located in Maumee OH. Since January I've worked with troubleshooting hardware, software and networking issues. I've created powershell and batch scripts to automize imaging process. Also created management tracking system for deployed assets' information, CISCO networking hardware, and static IPs on the network.

I am currently on the hunt for new opportunities to keep learning and expanding my knowledge, with the goal to become a software developer or a cybersecurity professional after graduation.
