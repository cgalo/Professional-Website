Hola!

In 2015, I started my undergraduate career at North Park University where I was a student-athlete majoring in Physics & Engineering. I started at UToledo in the Spring of 2017 and my expected graduation date is December 2020.

Since then, I have been able to be part of different groups that have helped me develop as a person and as a professional. Currently, I'm an active member of the professional business fraternity, Pi Sigma Epsilon. This is where I've had the honor to grow my business skills, create a bigger network of connections, and collaborate on projects to further improvement of our fraternity and its members. I am also developing the website for PSE for our chapter in UToledo, the website is being hosted through Google Cloud, where I deployed a LAMP (Linux, Apache, mySQL, PHP) stack to host the WordPress website. You can check the website here.

I was an intern at OnPoint Group a Salesforce Developer intern, located in Perrysburg OH. From July 2019 to December 2019 I've worked on developing custom applications on their Salesforce platform, which uses its proprietary object-oriented language called Apex. I also create lightning web-components using HTML, CSS, JavaScript and Apex. This internship gave me the opportunity to experience development first hand, while learning JavaScript and Apex through my projects.

I joined Quicken Loans and its family of companies in January 2020, as an intern in their Information Security family in Detroit MI. Here I had the pleasure to work in various inside teams such as Cloud Security, Compliance, and Software and Asset Licensing (SLAM) teams. I was able to work on the security compliance of QL's AWS resources, holding accountable our teams for their tag compliance in order to reduce cost and up time of EC2 and S3 resources. With the SLAM team I automated their Asset Management Application by pushing PowerShell scripts to our its server. These scripts automated the refreshing our users data, by collecting accurate information from Active Directory, and uploading back to the server. Also, sending weekly reports of unused licenses that could be recycle, given to a different user, or sent to review to stop paying for those licenses. By the end of my internship I was working with our Compliance team for the SOC (Service Organization Control) Type 2 audit. I assisted in the reviewing of controls and deliverables requested by our external auditors, as well as handling the evidence, and findings, of self-service deliverables.

Recently I joined another of Quicken Loans' companies, Nexsys Technologies, as a Software Engineer Intern. I will be working in their ClearSign application, on both their front-end, utilizing AngularJS, and their back-end, consisting of ASP.Net and C#.

I am currently on the hunt for new opportunities to keep learning and expanding my knowledge, with the goal to become a software engineer, information security engineer, or data scientist professional after graduation.
